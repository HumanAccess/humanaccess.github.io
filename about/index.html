About Human Access

My name's Phil, I left school in 1985 I took up the 'junior' position at my father's typesetting firm, a decade later the internet arrived in our studio and immediately hooked I dived into learning how to build web pages.

I worked professionally as a web developer through until late 2010 when for a variety of reasons I drifted away from the industry. Whilst I always continued to poke around with building websites, for the next 5-6 years I didn't pay much attention to the evolution of web development.

When I did get interested again around 2016 I became overwhelmed with how fast things had moved, and I pretty much burned out in a fireball trying to catch up, so I backed off again and just continued to poke about the edges again.

Over recent years I've picked up the pace again but this time I've taken a more defined approach by focusing on the things I was always passionate about as a young developer, CSS, HTML and web content accessibility, the other aspect is treating it primarily as a hobby to keep my brain active.

So if you've found you're way here and read this far, thanks for the interest in my profile and for checking out my website, I hope I've shared something here you've found useful.

Phil Brown
